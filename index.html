The Brown Bear

About Brown Bears

The brown bear(Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North Americs. Its conservation
status is currently Least Concern

There are many subspecies within the brown bear species,including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan
brown bear

Species

  • Arctos
  • Collarus
  • Horribilus
  • Nelsoni(extinct)

Features

Brown bears are not always completely brown. Some can be reddish or yellowish. They have very large,
curved claws and huge paws. Male brown bears are often 30% larger than female brown bears. They can
range from 5 feet to 9 feet head to toe.
